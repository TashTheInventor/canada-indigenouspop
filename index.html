Indigenous Populations in Canada: 2021 Census Analysis

Part 1: Majority of Canadians Are Non-Indigenous

This chart shows the national population breakdown from the 2021 Census. It highlights how the vast majority of Canadians are Non-Indigenous, while the groups, First Nations, Métis, and Inuit make up smaller proportions of the total.

Part 2: Geographic Distribution of Indigenous Populations

The map below shows how Indigenous populations are distributed across Canadian provinces and territories. You can toggle between different layers to view the overall percentage of Indigenous peoples, as well as specific counts for First Nations, Inuit, and Métis populations.

Part 3: Indigenous Adults Report Higher Food Insecurity and Poorer Mental Health

This interactive scatterplot explores the relationship between mental health and food insecurity across Indigenous and non-Indigenous groups. There are three frames switching between both sexes, males and females. The plots show consistent trends where Indigenous adults report higher food insecurity and poorer mental health.

Top Three Territories with the Largest Indigenous Populations

These three donut charts focus on the top 3 territories with the highest proportions of Indigenous residents. These 3 territories are Nunavut, the Northwest Territories, and Yukon. They show how Indigenous populations make up the majority in Nunavut and remain significant in the other northern regions.

Data Source: Statistics Canada, 2021 Census
